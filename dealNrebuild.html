The Waste Watcher!

“dealNrebuild” is a e-business concept, which breaks the traditional conception of managing construction and Demolition waste. Construction, demolition, restoration, and re modelling projects all produce a lot of building material waste.

Proper Management using the construction and demolition waste management rules and concept of 4R’s, we can promisingly turn “waste into resource” making an efforts towards sustainability and green building.

 

About dealNrebuild

Construction industry has always been in conflict with the environment protection agencies due to the lack of proper handling and management of Construction and Demolition Waste.
dealNrebuild is user friendly interface for sellers and buyers and will act as a mediator between seller and buyer to deal with C&D materials according to their preferred choice.
dealNrebuild, therefore provides a platform not only for the sellers and buyers but also to form a community for researchers, field or industry experts to access data, trends, technologies related to Construction and Demolition Waste Management and to lead a revolution towards sustainable living.

Concrete Waste: The most common type of Construction waste

Crushed reinforced concrete is in high demand as a recycled material.Crushed concrete aggregate the industry term for sand and gravel-can replace new aggregate in new concrete.
This substitution is a popular option because of the current global sand shortage .The sand necessary for making concrete comes from rivers, lakes and seashores-desert sand, eroded by wind rather than water, is too smooth to compact into stable concrete. The world uses 50 billion tons of sand and gravel aggregate every year, and our deltas and coastlines cannot sustain that output. Crushed concrete aggregate from construction and demolition projects is a highly appealing option.

Construction waste management in COVID-19 period


Waste reduction across all industries is fast becoming a priority, and particularly so in the construction industry. Waste materials such as concrete can be and are often recycled within developments themselves, used as foundational aggregate underneath buildings or as part of structures. However, during these challenging times, reducing the burden on restricted waste management services is crucial, allowing for resources to be optimally implemented where they are most needed.
